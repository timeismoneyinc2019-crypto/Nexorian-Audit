The Calculus of Deterministic Governance

Dennis Merritt
Nexorian Research Institute | February 2026
Abstract: This thesis introduces a formal mathematical framework for "Law Envelopes" as a thermodynamic barrier against stochastic drift in autonomous systems. By shifting governance from probabilistic application-layer guards to kernel-layer deterministic predicates, we define a new class of computation where rule violation is not merely forbidden but physical impossible within the execution environment.

I. Introduction: The Stochastic Fallacy

The contemporary landscape of Artificial Intelligence is dominated by probabilistic models. Large Language Models (LLMs) operate on a basis of high-dimensional "guessing," where the output is a statistical approximation of truth. In high-consequence environments—banking, national defense, medical orchestration—the margin for stochastic error is zero. Yet, current "safety" frameworks rely on secondary filters (Prompt Engineering, RLHF) that are inherently bypassable. We propose that true governance must be deterministic, not probabilistic.

II. The Law Envelope Predicate Logic

We define the Law Envelope ($E$) as a set of non-negotiable predicates ($P$) that guard every state transition ($S_n \to S_{n+1}$). In the Nexorian model, an instruction ($I$) is processed only if $\forall p \in E, p(S_n, I) = 1$. If any predicate fails, the transition is physically blocked at the hardware interrupt level. This ensures that the system state space is pruned of all non-compliant outcomes before execution energy is even allocated.

III. Thermodynamic Accounting (Epochs)

Traditional compute uses abstract tokens or time-outs. Nexorian introduces Thermodynamic Epochs, a unit of compute physically bound to the hardware entropy. By pre-allocating an Epoch limit to any agentic task, we create a mathematical ceiling for recursive reasoning. When the Epoch limit is hit, the system halts. This prevents "runaway" AI reasoning and ensures that energy expenditure is directly proportional to governed outcomes.

IV. Conclusion: Sovereignty through Constraint

Autonomy without constraint is liability. Sovereignty, in the digital age, is the ability to guarantee specific outcomes under all environmental conditions. Nexorian provides this guarantee by making the Law Envelope the fundamental boundary of the digital universe. We are not building agents that want to follow the rules; we are building agents that have to.
